## Abstract

> Phoneme recognition (PR), the problem of converting spoken language to a 
> sequence of phonemes, has seen much progress with the advent of deep learning 
> techniques, particularly with high-resource languages. However, in the case of 
> Filipino, a low-resource language, existing Filipino-specific PR systems are 
> primarily built on classical machine learning architectures that often struggle 
> to capture quirks such as stress or glottal stops. Furthermore, 
> language-specific Filipino speech processing models are often trained for 
> speech-to-grapheme transcription, rather than being trained and evaluated on 
> datasets annotated with phonetic labels, due primarily to a shortage of such 
> datasets. This study proposes to develop a Filipino-specific PR model with 
> deep-learning methods, fine-tuned from existing pre-trained architectures. This 
> PR model would be tested against existing Filipino or multilingual PR models on 
> phoneme-annotated datasets. The main contributions are thus as follows: (1) 
> establishing mixed-source phoneme-annotated datasets augmented by a 
> grapheme-to-phoneme (G2P) pipeline; (2) implementing a deep learning-based PR 
> model and evaluating its performance on baseline datasets; and (3) evaluating 
> its performance on a downstream task, namely mispronunciation detection and 
> diagnosis (MD\&D).
